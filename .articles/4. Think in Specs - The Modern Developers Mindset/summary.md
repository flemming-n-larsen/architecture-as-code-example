Your job is still software development — just without the coding. 🧑‍💻

A lot is being said about AI replacing developers. I think that misses the point entirely.

The developer's role has always evolved. Assembly abstracted away machine code. Managed runtimes abstracted away memory. Cloud abstracted away infrastructure. Agentic AI is just the next step — it abstracts away the coding itself.

What hasn't changed: software still moves through three layers.
Architecture → Design → Implementation.

AI is perfect at implementation. It recognizes patterns, follows instructions, and generates code at scale. But it is not intelligent — it is an automation engine. It has no stake in the outcome. You do.

Architecture and design still require judgment, intent, and accountability. That is where developers need to double down.

The mindset shift: think in specs first. Write the proposal. Detail the design. Define the tasks. Then let the AI execute.
