## Here's the problem I'm solving: ❓

Diagrams show *what* your system looks like. 🖼️ But they don't explain *why* you made those choices. Six months later,
someone asks: "Why did we choose UUIDs instead of auto-increment IDs for primary keys?" If that answer only lives in
someone's head, you've lost critical context. 🧠💭

This article shows how to layer **Architecture Decision Records (ADRs)** on top of your diagrams to capture the *why*
—the context, trade-offs, and rationale behind each decision. 📝🔍

## Why I wrote it: ✍️

As systems grow, traditional architecture documentation breaks down. Wikis rot. 📉 External diagram tools fall out of
sync. 🔄 Decisions disappear in PR noise. 🗑️ New team members waste weeks hunting for context. 🕵️‍♂️

Plain-text architecture as code fixes this. When your ADRs, C4 views, and domain models live in the repo—versioned with
Git, reviewed in PRs, and AI-readable—your documentation scales with the system. 📚🛠️

**Better yet:** use spec-driven development with OpenSpec to turn those architectural decisions into machine-readable
specifications. 🤖 Your **AI agents can then help keep architecture, specs, and code continuously in sync**—validating
implementation against decisions and surfacing improvements automatically. 🔄✅

Architecture becomes executable, auditable, and self-maintaining. 🏆

The article includes a complete, ready-to-use folder structure, MADR templates, and a working example repo you can fork.
